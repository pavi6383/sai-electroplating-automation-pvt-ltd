Semi-Automatic Plants

Enhance Efficiency with Semi-Automatic Electroplating Plants

Semi-automatic electroplating plants combine manual and automated processes to offer a cost-effective and versatile solution for plating. These plants feature automated control systems like PLCs to manage key parameters such as temperature and current, while manual handling is used for loading and unloading workpieces. Automation elements, including conveyors and agitation systems, improve efficiency and consistency, while adjustable settings and monitoring systems ensure high-quality results.

Enhance Efficiency with Semi-Automatic Electroplating Plants

These plants are widely used across industries. In automotive, they enhance durability and appearance of components, such as through chrome plating. Electronics benefit from improved conductivity in parts like connectors, while aerospace applications require high-performance coatings. Jewelry and medical device manufacturing utilize plating for aesthetic finishes and increased biocompatibility. In industrial machinery, these plants provide wear-resistant coatings to extend component life.

Manual Plant
Manual Electroplating Plant

A manual electroplating plant offers hands-on control for small to medium-scale production, ideal for industries like jewelry and small electronics. It is cost-effective, providing lower initial investment and flexibility for custom plating needs. Operators have direct control, ensuring high precision and quality. The plants adaptability allows for various plating techniques and materials, while its straightforward design simplifies maintenance. Overall, manual electroplating plants combine affordability with flexibility and precision.

Anodizing Plants (Type I, II, III)

An anodizing plant enhances aluminum and other metals by thickening their natural oxide layer through an electrochemical process. This boosts corrosion and wear resistance and improves appearance. The plant features anodizing tanks (made from polypropylene or fiberglass), rectifiers for DC power, rinsing stations, temperature controls, filtration systems, and drying equipment. The process operates at temperatures between 15°C and 25°C using electrolytes like sulfuric or chromic acid.

Anodizing provides benefits such as better corrosion resistance, wear resistance, and aesthetics, while also enhancing paint adhesion and reducing maintenance. It is widely used in aerospace, automotive, electronics, architecture, and consumer goods industries. The process allows precise control over the anodic layer thickness (5 to 25 micrometers) and includes post-anodizing treatments like sealing and dyeing for customized finishes.

Passivation Line Plants

A passivation line plant applies chemical treatments to metal surfaces to enhance corrosion resistance and durability. Types include Chromium Passivation Plants, using chromium-based chemicals for stainless steel; Nitric Acid Passivation Plants, employing nitric acid to clean and protect; Organic Passivation Plants, using organic compounds for specific needs; and Zinc Passivation Plants, which apply a zinc coating followed by passivation for improved resistance.

Benefits and Features

Benefits of passivation include increased corrosion resistance, durability, and improved appearance, with reduced maintenance costs and better performance. Features of these plants include corrosion-resistant treatment chambers, precise control systems, rinsing and drying units, and efficient waste management. Passivation line plants are essential for extending the lifespan and functionality of metal components across industries like aerospace, automotive, and manufacturing.
